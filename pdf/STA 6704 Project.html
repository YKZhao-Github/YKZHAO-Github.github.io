My dataset is an NBA basketball dataset from Kaggle.com. (https://www.kaggle.com/wyattowalsh/basketball). It has comprehensive stats from the first NBA season (1946-1947) to now, including games, players, and team stats. The initial problem I wanted to explore involved looking at individual players, but as I explored the data, I switched to looking at teams instead. I wanted to explore this particular dataset because I love the game of basketball and I am motivated to contribute to the game with data mining.

Here is the intial reading of the data. It is an sqlite file with multiple tables, so I read it in using RSQLite and checked the tables using DBI

#read sqlite file

I then isolated the tables so I could explore the data individually.

I quickly realized here that there was not enough in-game player data for me to focus on specific players. General stats like how many points a player scored in a particular game were not well represented in any of the tables. Many of the tables here were extra statistics that are not useful on their own. For instance, player salary can be useful, but only if I have enough in-game player statistics to hold it to.

After parsing through the tables, I decided to switch my focus on analyzing team data instead of individual players.

The Game table was perfect for that. It had team stats on individual games. The other tables with team data in them were extraneous.

Here, I run the boost and look at the variable importance. I expect to see Away Points (PTS_AWAY) being high and Away 3 pointers (FG3M_AWAY) being low. Running it a few times does yield what I expect. This is because the team who scores more points wins the game. For the 3 pointers, I expected it to have low importance because the years for the test data (2015-now) has much more 3 pointers in the game style.

#run boost

For my model, I ran random forest several times with 2-4 mtry and many different trees. I always got around 20-25% OOB error and around 70ish accuracy.

Looking at the variable importance, I believe 3 pointers were the biggest difference in accounting for the error. Running the boosting on variable importance always showed FG3M_AWAY, the number of three pointers away teams made, at or towards the bottom.

Perhaps in the future, I would run this with different models and also with a different mix of variables. There were many in the game table that I did not use. To me, based on domain knowledge, the ones I used were some of the most important, especially 3 pointers, given how few 3 pointers were taken in the training data vs how many were taken in the test set.
